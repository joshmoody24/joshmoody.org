Weird glitch in the news matrix today.

Google the phrase
["100 terawatts of processing power"](https://www.google.com/search?q=%22100+terawatts+of+processing+power%22&tbs=cdr:1,cd_min:4/28/2026,cd_max:4/30/2026)
on April 30, 2026 and you'll find articles
on Elon Musk's new SpaceX pay package. One phrase caught my eye:

> [Elon Musk's pay package] has additional incentives of 60.4
> million in restricted shares if SpaceX… operates space-based
> data centers with at least **100 terawatts of processing power**.
>
> - [Gizmodo](https://gizmodo.com/elon-musk-will-earn-a-company-bonus-if-he-drops-a-million-colonists-on-mars-2000752661)
>   (original source is
>   [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/spacex-ties-musk-compensation-mars-colonization-goal-2026-04-28/),
>   but it's paywalled)

Terawatt is not a unit of *processing* power. It's a unit of power, as in "how fast energy is being used."
The entire world currently consumes electricity at a rate of 3.5 terawatts.<FootnoteRef number={1} /> That's one hungry space computer.

<Footnote number={1}>
  [Electricity consumption was 28,000 terawatt-hours in 2023.](https://www.iea.org/data-and-statistics/data-tools/energy-statistics-data-browser?country=WORLD&fuel=Energy%20consumption&indicator=TotElecCons) Extrapolating 2026 consumption as 31,000 terawatt-hours and dividing by (365.25 × 24) results in 3.536 terawatts on average.
</Footnote>

Maybe they<FootnoteRef number={2} /> meant "teraflops," but 100 teraflops of processing power is roughly one [NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5090 graphics card](https://www.techpowerup.com/gpu-specs/geforce-rtx-5090.c4216#:~:text=FP32%20(float)-,104.8%20TFLOPS,-FP64%20(double)).

<Footnote number={2}>
  "They" being either the SpaceX board or the Reuters journalist. Not sure which.
</Footnote>

So Elon Musk is getting billions of dollars in shares for either 1) eclipsing the entire planet's power usage, or 2) shooting a gaming PC into space. This story makes no sense.
